About Me

Hello! I'm Sahil Maurya, a recent M.Tech graduate (1st Rank) from the prestigious Indian Institute of Information Technology, Guwahati.

My journey is defined by resilience and passion. Hailing from a small village in Uttar Pradesh, I overcame significant academic setbacks to secure First Class Honors in my B.Tech and eventually topped my Master's batch. My research focuses on Digital Design Architecture, Hardware Security, and Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC).

Under the transformative guidance of Dr. Babita Jajodia, I specialized in optimizing hardware for cryptosystems and exploring In-Memory Computing architectures. Beyond the lab, I am an avid cricket player, storyteller, and explorer of new cultures.

Key Projects

Study Of Large Integer Multiplication For Cryptosystem

Verilog HDL | FPGA | Toom-Cook Algorithm | Xilinx Vivado

Designed 32-bit Toom-Cook-2.5 and Toom-Cook-3 multiplication algorithms for cryptosystems. Implemented on FPGA and performed comparative analysis on Area, LUT, Delay, Frequency, and Power consumption.

Area & Power Efficient Approximate Modular Multiplier (R-LWE)

ASIC Design | 45nm Library | Cadence Genus | Post-Quantum Crypto

Designed a modular multiplier for medium & high security levels of R-LWE Cryptosystem on 45nm ASIC library. Achieved 36% area reduction and 23% power reduction compared to state-of-the-art exact multipliers.
